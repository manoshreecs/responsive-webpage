Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam – Missile Man & People's President

Dr. Kalam, known as India's “Missile Man” and former President, inspiring the nation.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 Oct 1931 – 27 Jul 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002–2007 :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering before contributing to India's civilian space and military missile programs at DRDO and ISRO :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Known as the “Missile Man of India,” he led key projects: the SLV‑III satellite launch vehicle, the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles, and played a pivotal role in the 1998 Pokhran‑II nuclear tests :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Elected President in 2002 with support across India's political spectrum, he earned the title “People’s President” for his humility and connection with the public :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}. Post‑presidency, he inspired millions—especially youth—through books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020 :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

He passed away from cardiac arrest in 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong; India observed seven days of mourning :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}. His legacy lives on—honoured with the Bharat Ratna (1997) and memorialized in Rameswaram with the Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam Memorial :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.
